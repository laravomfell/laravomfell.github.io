Differences in police officer experience and attitude are not the origin of ethnic bias in stop and search

Lara Vomfell , Neil Stewart
October 2019
Code PDF

Abstract

Black and Asian people are more likely to be stopped and searched than White People. We explore the antecedents of this ethnic bias using the records of 53,000 search decisions following a panel of 2,200 individual police officers over 3.5 years. We show that officer experience with crime suspects has no effect on stop and search bias. In other words, the ethnic composition of crime suspects an officer experiences is completely unrelated to the ethnic composition of people they stop and search. We also show that biased attitudes in an officer’s stop and search estimated in one random half of the data predicts nothing about their bias in the other half. That is, there are no stable differences between officers in attitudes impacting their stop and search behaviour. We need to look elsewhere, beyond officer differences in experience and attitude, for the origins of and remedies for bias in stop and search.
